## 1. Niche Selection & CPM Analysis

Choosing the right niche is the single most important decision in this playbook. We need a niche that sits at the intersection of three criteria: high advertiser CPM, strong alignment with AI-assisted content production, and genuine passion from you as the creator. Based on current 2025-2026 data, the following table ranks the top five highest-CPM niches on YouTube [1] [2] [3].

| Rank | Niche | Estimated CPM | Competition | AI-Content Fit |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Personal Finance & Investing** | $15 - $45 | Very High | High. Data-driven, chart-heavy content is easy to script and visualize with AI. |
| 2 | **Insurance & Legal** | $10 - $38 | Medium | Medium. Complex topics simplified by AI, but audience is narrow. |
| 3 | **Software & Technology (SaaS/B2B)** | $10 - $35 | High | Very High. Tool reviews, tutorials, and comparisons are AI's sweet spot. |

| 5 | **Health & Fitness (Biohacking/Specific Conditions)** | $8 - $25 | High | Medium. AI can synthesize research, but requires careful disclaimers. |

### Our Selection: Software & Technology (AI Agents, SaaS, Vibe Coding)

We are going deep into the **Software & Technology** niche, specifically the sub-niche of **AI Agents, SaaS Tools, and Vibe Coding**. This is the right call for three reasons.

First, **the CPM is excellent.** Technology advertisers (SaaS companies, cloud providers, developer tools) pay $10-$35 CPM because their customer lifetime value is enormous. A single B2B software customer can be worth $10,000+, so they bid aggressively for attention on YouTube [2].

Second, **you are a practitioner.** You build all day long. You have tested Manus, Cursor, Claude Code, and dozens of other tools. Most tech YouTubers are surface-level reviewers who read the landing page and click around the UI. You can show real terminal output, real builds, and real results. That authenticity is irreplaceable and cannot be faked by competitors.

Third, **the timing is perfect.** "Vibe coding" entered the mainstream vocabulary in early 2025 and is now one of the fastest-growing search terms on YouTube [4]. AI agents (like Manus, Devin, and Claude Code) are the hottest topic in tech. Forbes, Zapier, and TechCrunch are all publishing weekly roundups of new AI coding tools [5] [6]. We are riding a wave, not trying to create one.

## 2. Channel Branding & Positioning

### The Core Identity: The Builder, Not The Reviewer

Your positioning statement is the foundation of every piece of content we create:

> "I don't review AI tools. I build with them. This channel is the open-source playbook for orchestrating AI agents, SaaS, and automation to build profitable systems."

This single sentence separates you from 95% of tech channels. It implies depth, practicality, and generosity. You are not gatekeeping knowledge behind a $997 course. You are building in public.

### The Marketing Moat: Open Orchestration vs. Walled Gardens

Many competitors in the AI/SaaS space use a **walled-garden approach**. They tease a workflow in a free video but hide the actual prompts, configuration files, and integration steps behind a paid community or course. Our counter-advantage is **open, platform-agnostic orchestration**. We give away the exact workflows, prompts, and blueprints for free. We show how different tools (Cursor + Supabase + Vercel, or Claude Code + Make.com + Airtable) work together across platforms. By being the open-source architect of the AI tools space, you build immense trust. The monetization comes from YouTube AdSense (high CPM), affiliate revenue from the tools you genuinely use, and high-ticket sponsorships from SaaS companies who want access to your highly technical, high-intent audience.

## 3. YouTube Strategy

### Channel Setup Essentials

When creating the channel, ensure the following are configured from day one. The channel description should be keyword-rich and clearly state the value proposition (e.g., "Zero-touch automation workflows, AI agent tutorials, and honest SaaS reviews for builders and developers"). Create 3-4 initial playlists to organize content: "Vibe Coding Tutorials," "AI Agent Comparisons," "Zero-Touch Workflows," and "Tool Reviews." Upload a professional banner that matches the visual identity described above. Set the channel trailer to your first published video.

### Content Format

The primary format is **8-12 minute screen-recorded tutorials and comparisons**. This length is optimal because it allows for 1-2 mid-roll ad placements (which significantly increase RPM) while being short enough to maintain high Average View Duration (AVD). The structure for every video follows a proven retention framework:

| Timestamp | Section | Purpose | Duration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0:00 - 0:30 | **The Hook** | Visual proof of the end result. Show the finished build or the shocking comparison stat. | 30 sec |
| 0:30 - 1:30 | **The Problem** | Articulate the pain point the viewer has. Create empathy. | 60 sec |
| 1:30 - 8:00 | **The Solution (Core Content)** | The step-by-step walkthrough. This is the meat of the video. | 6-7 min |
| 8:00 - 9:30 | **The Results / Takeaway** | Show the measurable outcome. Summarize the key insight. | 90 sec |
| 9:30 - 10:00 | **Call to Action** | Subscribe, download the blueprint, watch the next video. | 30 sec |

### Upload Cadence

Publish **2 videos per week**, every Monday and Thursday. Consistency is the single most important factor for the YouTube algorithm. Publishing on a predictable schedule trains the algorithm to promote your content and trains your audience to expect it. Two videos per week is aggressive but achievable with our AI workflow because Manus handles the research and scripting, leaving you to focus on production and publishing.

### Hitting YouTube Partner Program (YPP) Requirements

The YPP requires **1,000 subscribers** and **4,000 valid public watch hours** in the last 12 months (or 10 million Shorts views in 90 days) [7]. With 2 videos per week averaging 8-10 minutes each, you need approximately 3,000-5,000 views per video to hit 4,000 watch hours within 3-6 months. The strategy to achieve this is to focus on **search-driven evergreen content** (e.g., "Claude Code vs Devin," "Best AI Agents 2026") that ranks in YouTube search and generates consistent daily views. Supplement this with **YouTube Shorts** (15-60 second clips repurposed from your long-form videos) to drive rapid subscriber growth.

### Thumbnail & Title Strategy

Titles should be curiosity-driven, contain the primary keyword, and stay under 60 characters. The formula is: **[Specific Claim] + [Emotional Trigger] + [Year/Qualifier]**. Examples include "Claude Code vs Devin: The Brutal Truth (2026)" and "I Replaced My $2,000/mo Agency with This AI Stack." Thumbnails should use high contrast, minimal text (under 5 words), and a strong focal point. Always create 2 thumbnail variations and use YouTube's built-in A/B testing feature. Check results after 3 hours and switch to the winner.

## 4. X (Twitter) Strategy

X is the real-time engagement engine that amplifies your YouTube content, builds your personal brand, and generates its own ad revenue.

### Profile Optimization

Your X bio should mirror your YouTube positioning: "Building automated systems with AI agents. Sharing the exact playbooks. No walled gardens." Pin your highest-performing thread or a link to your latest YouTube video. Use the same visual identity (dark theme, neon accents) for your profile picture and banner to create instant brand recognition across platforms.

### Content Types That Drive Impressions

The X algorithm in 2026 heavily rewards **long-form threads**, **visual content** (screenshots, short video clips), and **engagement bait** (polls, questions, contrarian takes) [8]. Your content mix should follow the 70/30 rule: 70% of your time on X should be spent replying thoughtfully to large accounts in the AI/SaaS niche, and 30% should be your own original posts [9]. This is because replies to large accounts expose your profile to their audience, which is the fastest organic growth lever on X.

### X Ad Revenue Sharing: Requirements & Strategy

X's Creator Revenue Sharing program requires an **X Premium subscription** (paid verification), a minimum of **500 followers**, and at least **5 million organic impressions** on your posts within the last 3 months [10] [11]. Importantly, the revenue is generated from ads shown in the replies to your posts, and the engagement that counts most comes from other Premium/Verified users. This means your content strategy should target the tech/builder community, which has a disproportionately high rate of X Premium subscriptions.

### The YouTube-X Flywheel

The cross-pollination between YouTube and X is the engine that accelerates growth on both platforms. Every YouTube script gets converted into an X thread (Manus handles this automatically). Every X thread that performs well gets expanded into a full YouTube video. Short video clips (15-30 seconds) from YouTube are posted natively on X to drive traffic back to the full video. This creates a virtuous cycle where each platform feeds the other.

*(Visual: B-roll of someone looking frustrated at a ChatGPT interface. Cut to a diagram showing a broken feedback loop between ChatGPT and a code editor.)*

Here is the problem with how 90% of people use AI to code. They open ChatGPT, they paste in a prompt, they copy the code, and they paste it into their editor.

True vibe coding requires orchestration. It requires an AI agent that has full context of your entire codebase, your database schema, and your deployment environment. It needs to be able to read the errors itself and fix them autonomously.

That is why we don't use web interfaces. We use agents integrated directly into our environment.

### [2:30 - 5:00] THE STACK

*(Visual: Screen recording of opening Cursor. Show the AI chat panel on the side. Then cut to the Supabase dashboard showing a clean, empty database.)*

This is the stack.

First, the brain: **Cursor**. It's a fork of VS Code, but it has AI built natively into the editor. More importantly, it has "Composer" mode, which allows the AI to edit multiple files simultaneously and run terminal commands.

Second, the backend: **Supabase**. It's an open-source Firebase alternative. We use it because it provides instant APIs, authentication, and a Postgres database that our AI agent can easily understand and interact with.

Third, the deployment: **Vercel**. It connects directly to our GitHub repo and deploys our app every time the AI pushes code.

*(Visual: Split screen. Left side is Cursor generating a SQL schema. Right side is Supabase automatically updating its tables.)*

Watch this. I'm not going to write a single line of SQL. I'm going to tell Cursor: "Create a database schema for a project management tool. We need users, projects, and tasks. Generate the SQL and apply it to my Supabase instance."

Because Cursor has terminal access, it uses the Supabase CLI to push the migration. Zero touch. The database is ready.

### [5:00 - 8:00] THE BUILD

*(Visual: Screen recording of Cursor generating React components. The user is typing natural language commands like "Make the dashboard look like Stripe" or "Add a dark mode toggle.")*

Now for the frontend. This is where the "vibe" comes in. I don't care about the specific React hooks or the Tailwind classes. I care about the architecture and the user experience.

I tell the agent: "Scaffold a Next.js dashboard. Connect it to the Supabase tables we just created. Use Tailwind for styling, and make it look like the Stripe dashboard."

*(Visual: Fast forward through the code generation. Show the terminal running the local server. The app appears in the browser.)*

It took 45 seconds. The app is running locally. It has authentication. It pulls data from our database.

But it's not perfect. The alignment on the sidebar is off.

In the old days, you'd inspect the element, find the CSS class, and fix it. With vibe coding, you just highlight the sidebar in Cursor and say, "Fix the padding here, it looks cramped." The agent understands the context, finds the file, and applies the fix.

You are no longer a programmer. You are an executive producer. You are directing the AI.

### [8:00 - 9:30] DEPLOYMENT & THE MARKETING MOAT

*(Visual: Screen recording of pushing the code to GitHub. Cut to the Vercel dashboard showing the deployment building and turning green.)*

We push to GitHub. Vercel sees the commit and deploys it globally. We just built and shipped a full-stack SaaS application without writing a single line of code manually.

This is the power of open orchestration. You don't need to be locked into a proprietary, walled-garden app builder that charges you $100 a month and owns your data. You can use open-source tools, orchestrated by AI, to own your entire stack.
